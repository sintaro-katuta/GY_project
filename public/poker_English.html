What is Poker?

Poker is a genre of game played with playing cards.
It is a game characterized by psychological warfare.
Players create bands (roles or means) with five cards and compete on the strength of their roles.
When playing as gambling, cash is exchanged for chips, and the chips earned by the player are used as income.

Play by yourself and earn chips.
It is not a gamble like baccarat, where you predict the winners of other players.
While most gambling is between the player and the house, in the case of poker it is between the customers sitting at the table, and the house receives an "ante" or a percentage of the winning chips as a commission.
Unlike other casino games, the promoter does not participate in gambling, but rather provides a table for guests to compete with each other and services such as game progression.

dealer

In casinos, there is one dedicated dealer employed by the casino at each poker table, and this dealer deals the cards in every game.
In tournaments and large private games, "amusement dealers" are responsible for game progression and other activities.
In private games that are not large (e.g., in ordinary homes), one of the players often doubles as the dealer.
There is no set rule as to which player becomes the dealer, but usually one of the following four methods is used to determine the dealer

  1. The person to the left of the dealer in the previous game becomes the dealer in the next game.
  2. The winner of the previous game becomes the dealer of the next game.
  3. A fixed specific player becomes the dealer.
  4. As in casinos, the person who does not play becomes the dealer.
Which rules will be used to determine the dealer will be discussed among the players in advance.
In flop (community card) poker, a plate marked "DEALER" is often moved clockwise from game to game (this plate is called the "dealer button") as a standard for starting to deal cards and forcing bets.

pot

In poker, the area near the center of the table is used as a place to place betting chips. This place to place chips is called the pot.
Players place their bets close to themselves in the pot, distinguishable from chips bet by other players.
At the end of each deal, players may not put chips back in their hands once they have bet, except when the winner of the deal receives them.

anty

The participation fee, which everyone participating in play must pay a certain amount before each game begins, is called an ante.
Whether or not an ante is required is determined in advance by the game.
Stud games are usually set up to pay ante, but this is not always necessary in other games.
The paid ante is collected in the pot (some houses collect the ante as a commission and do not enter the pot) and is won by the winner along with subsequent multipliers.

bet

In poker, there are several periods during the game for betting (wagering).
Each of these periods is called a betting interval (or betting round).
Each betting interval shall be as follows
The betting interval starts with one of the players (the player to the left of the dealer button in a flop game) and runs clockwise.
In poker, a player can get out of the game (also expressed as fold, down, or drop) at any time during the game.
The player who folds is considered to have lost the deal.
The player participating in the deal who has not yet folded is called the active player. Thereafter, the deal is made by the active player only.
When a player folds, he or she cannot get back the chips wagered on the deal.
The player who folded keeps the chips he or she bet on the deal, and the winner of the deal gets them at the end of the deal.
Example: If you bet 25 points and then get out as a result of a raise from your opponent, your initial bet of 25 chips goes into the pot.
Each betting interval is divided into two parts: before and after the opening bet.
Before the opening bet, each active player takes one of the following actions when it is his/her turn.

  • Check.
  • Opening bets.
  • Fold.

check

A check is to pass the bet for that round.
Instead of declaring "check," the player who checks can also lightly tap the table a couple of times to indicate his or her intention to check.
An opening bet is a chip bet on the pot.
If everyone checks, the game proceeds to the next step.
After the opening bet has been made, each active player takes one of the following actions when it is his/her turn

  • Calling.
  • Raise.
  • Fold.
The active player must either multiply or fold by the same or more amount as the previous player when it is his/her turn.
Betting the same amount of chips as the previous player is called a call, and raising the bet is called a raise.
The betting interval continues until all active players have checked, or until all active players have equaled the amount of chips they have put up.

Upper limit of bets

The type of game in which a limit is set on the amount that can be bet or raised so that the game does not go to the blue ceiling is called a limit game.
Conversely, a game in which no limit is set at all is called a no-limit game (sometimes abbreviated as "NL," "N/L," etc.).
There are variations on how to limit the value of the raise, such as

Fixed limit

Only a certain pre-determined amount can be increased.
When simply described as "limit," this "fixed limit" is often referred to.

Spread limit

For example, "any amount can be increased in the range of $1 to $5," with a certain range.
It is sometimes employed in low money games, but rarely in tournaments.

Pot limit

The rule is that the total amount in the pot is the maximum amount that can be bet.

No limit

A rule that does not set a limit on the amount of money that can be bet or raised.
