What is Sic Bo?

Dasho or daisai (Cantonese: taisai) is a betting game in which three dice are used to predict the total number of rolls, etc.
Also called Sic-Bo.
This is mainly done in casinos in Asia (especially Macau).

basic rule

Since three dice are used, the minimum total number of rolls is 3 (1's) and the maximum number is 18 (6's), and the total number of rolls of the three dice is between 4 and 10 (small) and between 11 and 17 (large). The minimum number of 3 and the maximum number of 18 are classified as neither "big" nor "small" according to the rules of "big" and "small".
Unlike relatively similar roulette, the dice are contained in a fully sealed machine container with a lid a double lid with an opaque removable outer lid and a fixed transparent inner lid. The dice are shuffled by the machine with the outer lid removed (the dice are not accessible when the outer lid is removed), the bets are placed by placing chips on the table in anticipation of the payout after the payout is determined (as in roulette, the placement of chips indicates anticipation), and then the outer lid is opened to disclose the payout.
